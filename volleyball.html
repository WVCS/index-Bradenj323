    Rotations
    Rotation: Graphic Explanation
    5-1
    		The 5-1 rotation is the most commonly run in professional play due to its versatility. In this rotation, the setter plays front row (up on the net) for a few rotations and is allowed to block and dump (a sneaky setter attack). When the setter is in the front, the opposite plays defence in the back row and hits D ball.
    6-2
    		The 6-2 rotation typically has a setter play backrow during the entire game. When the setter rotates to the front, they are typically swapped out for an opposite. This allows their to be three hitters up on the net at all times giving the setter and offence more options for a front row offence. This rotation is very strong offensively, however it had less room for setter creativity since they are playing back row the entire time. It also does not allow the setter to block or dump.
